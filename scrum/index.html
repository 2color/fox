Scrum in a Nutshell

Scrum is an iterative and incremental agile software development framework for managing product development. Scrum involves planning and estimating workloads, and then trying to achieve those estimated goals, and ship a Product Increment at the end.

There are three core roles in the Scrum framework: the Product Owner, representing the stakeholders, the actual Development Team, responsible for the technical implementations, and the Scrum Master, who manages the overall Scrum process and environment, thereby enabling the Development Team to be self-organizing.

The work cycle is called a sprint, and it typically lasts 2-4 weeks. Before the sprint begins an estimate and ‘sprint backlog’ is created. A sprint backlog is a list of features that are to be included in the finished product.

Scrum workflow:
Planning
Daily standup meeting
What did I do yesterday that helped the development team meet the sprint goal?
What will I do today to help the development team meet the sprint goal?
Do I see any impediment that prevents me or the development team from meeting the sprint goal?

Sprint Review (post-sprint)
Reviews the work that was completed and the planned work that was not completed
Presents the completed work to the stakeholders (a.k.a. the demo)
Sprint retrospective
Team reflects on the past sprint
Identifies and agrees on continuous process improvement actions

Use of Scrum by the Fox team

Team Fox engaged in an accelerated version of the Scrum framework; however, instead of 2 week sprints, we were limited to using 30 minute sprints. Other than that, we strived to adhere to the principles of Scrum as tightly as possible.
